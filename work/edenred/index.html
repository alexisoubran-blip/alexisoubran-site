How Edenred made B2B lead generation work on TikTok.

A creator-led TikTok Lead Ads system that made B2B demand feel native without losing conversion discipline.

The business problem

B2B lead generation on TikTok sounds like a bad idea until the system is built correctly. Edenred needed to increase qualified conversion volume in a category where the audience was not actively shopping in the classic sense, but could be educated, engaged, and converted through native content.

The challenge was not simply to generate reach. The challenge was to make a B2B value proposition feel human enough for TikTok while keeping the acquisition model disciplined enough for performance review.

The system

1. Creator-led B2B relevance

The campaign used creators to translate Edenred's value proposition into relatable business and workplace contexts. Instead of forcing a corporate message into a short-form platform, the system made the problem feel recognizable: employee benefits, operational simplicity, and business value explained through content people could actually watch.

2. Lead Ads to reduce friction

The conversion path happened inside TikTok through Lead Ads, reducing unnecessary landing-page friction and allowing faster testing of messaging, creator angles, and audience segments.

3. Native creative with performance discipline

The content had to feel native, but the operating system was not judged by vibes. Every angle was evaluated through conversion volume, cost efficiency, lead quality, and ability to scale.

4. CPA governance

The system was read on conversion volume, cost and acquisition efficiency, not just engagement. B2B content can entertain, but finance still prefers math. Tragic, but useful.

The results

  • +259% conversion volume.
  • -65% CPI reduction.
  • 2.7M unique reach.
  • 8.6M video views.
  • 543 total conversions.
  • $22.3 USD CPA.

Operating lesson: B2B does not fail on TikTok because the platform is wrong. It fails when brands bring boardroom language into a platform built for human pattern recognition.

Why it mattered

The Edenred system proved that B2B lead generation can work inside creator-led short-form environments when the message is translated into native platform behavior and measured with conversion discipline.

The key was not pretending TikTok behaves like search. The key was assigning the channel the right role: discovery, education, trust creation and low-friction lead capture.

Reusable pattern

This system applies to B2B brands whose buyers are not always actively searching but can be activated through a clear problem, human explanation and low-friction conversion path.

Best-fit categories include employee benefits, SaaS, HR tech, fintech, business services, education, healthcare-adjacent solutions and any B2B product where the buyer needs explanation before conversion.
